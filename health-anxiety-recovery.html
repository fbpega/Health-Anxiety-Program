Quick Start

Why This Works

Health anxiety thrives on avoidance. Every time you check, reassure-seek, or avoid a sensation — your brain learns "that was dangerous." Exposure + sitting with discomfort teaches your nervous system the truth: sensations are uncomfortable, not deadly.

Daily Practice

Evidence-based exercises for health anxiety recovery.

💓
Intentional Sensation Induction
Deliberately provoke the sensations you fear, on your terms.
Interoceptive Exposure

This teaches your brain that the sensations themselves are not dangerous — you created them deliberately and survived.

  • 1Choose one sensation: fast heartbeat (run in place 60s), shortness of breath (breathe through a straw for 30s), or dizziness (spin in a chair 10x).
  • 2Deliberately trigger it. Go all in — the more you commit, the stronger the learning.
  • 3Notice the sensations. Label them: "fast heartbeat," "chest tightness." Do not judge.
  • 4Sit with it for 2–3 minutes. Do NOT reassure yourself it will pass. Just observe.
  • 5Notice when it subsides. Log how your anxiety level changed.
🧠
Catastrophic Thought Defusion
Unhook from "what if" spirals without reassurance-seeking.
Cognitive Defusion

The goal is not to disprove the thought — it's to stop treating it as a fact that requires action.

  • 1Write down the exact thought: "This chest pain means I'm having a heart attack."
  • 2Reframe as a story: "I notice I'm having the thought that this chest pain means I'm having a heart attack."
  • 3Say it out loud in a silly voice, or extremely slowly. This breaks the emotional grip.
  • 4Ask: "Has this thought been helpful? Is it based on evidence, or is it anxiety talking?"
  • 5Resist looking it up, asking anyone, or seeking reassurance. Uncertainty is tolerable.
🌊
Sensation Surfing
Ride the wave of discomfort without fighting or fleeing.
Mindful Acceptance
  • 1When a sensation arises, resist the urge to make it go away. Instead, get curious.
  • 2Describe it like a scientist: "Pressure in the center of my chest, about 4cm wide. Mild pulsing. Intensity: 6/10."
  • 3Track it: is it getting stronger, weaker, moving? Treat it like weather you're observing.
  • 4Breathe normally. Not to calm down — just breathe naturally and keep observing.
  • 5Stay with it for at least 10 minutes. Notice it ebbs and flows — it was never static.
🚫
Reassurance Delay & Resist
Break the checking and reassurance cycle.
Behavioral

Checking and reassurance-seeking are the fuel of health anxiety. Every check relieves briefly, then demands more.

  • 1Identify today's urge: Googling symptoms, checking pulse, asking someone for reassurance.
  • 2Set a delay: "I will wait 20 minutes before doing anything."
  • 3During those 20 minutes, continue what you were doing. Don't fight the urge, just delay it.
  • 4If anxiety peaked and passed in that time — you've proven you can tolerate uncertainty.
  • 5Increase the delay each day: 20min → 1hr → 3hr → 24hr → never.
📋
Worst Case Acceptance Script
Face the feared outcome directly, without escape.
Imaginal Exposure

The goal is not reassurance — it's to reduce the emotional charge of the feared scenario through repeated exposure to it.

  • 1Write out your worst fear in detail: "I have chest pain and it turns out to be a heart attack. I collapse..."
  • 2Read it aloud, slowly, 3 times. Let the anxiety rise. Don't stop or reassure yourself.
  • 3Each time you read it, notice if your anxiety peaks and then — gradually — decreases.
  • 4Repeat daily for 2 weeks with the same script. The fear loses its power through repetition.
  • 5This is not about convincing yourself it won't happen. It's about tolerating the uncertainty.
