Daily Practice

Evidence-based exercises. Mark one done to tick your daily plan.

💓
Intentional Sensation Induction
Deliberately provoke the sensations you fear, on your terms.
Interoceptive Exposure

This teaches your brain that sensations are not dangerous — you created them and survived.

  • 1Choose a sensation: fast heartbeat (run in place 60s), breathlessness (straw breathing 30s), dizziness (spin 10x).
  • 2Trigger it fully. The more you commit, the stronger the learning.
  • 3Label what you feel: "fast heartbeat," "chest tightness." Observe without judging.
  • 4Sit with it 2–3 minutes. Do NOT reassure yourself. Just observe.
  • 5Notice when it subsides naturally. Log it.
🧠
Catastrophic Thought Defusion
Unhook from "what if" spirals without reassurance-seeking.
Cognitive Defusion
  • 1Write the exact thought: "This chest pain means I'm having a heart attack."
  • 2Reframe: "I notice I'm having the thought that this chest pain means a heart attack."
  • 3Say it aloud in a silly voice or extremely slowly. Breaks the emotional grip.
  • 4Ask: "Is this helpful? Evidence-based, or anxiety talking?"
  • 5Resist Googling, checking, or asking anyone for reassurance.
🌊
Sensation Surfing
Ride the wave of discomfort without fighting or fleeing.
Mindful Acceptance
  • 1When a sensation arises, get curious instead of alarmed.
  • 2Describe it like a scientist: location, size, intensity, texture.
  • 3Track it — growing, fading, moving? Treat it like weather you're observing.
  • 4Breathe normally. Not to calm down — just naturally.
  • 5Stay 10+ minutes. Notice it ebbs and flows. It was never static.
🚫
Reassurance Delay & Resist
Break the checking and reassurance cycle.
Behavioral
  • 1Identify today's urge: Googling, checking pulse, asking for reassurance.
  • 2Set a delay: "I will wait 20 minutes before acting."
  • 3Continue what you were doing. Don't fight the urge — delay it.
  • 4If anxiety peaked and passed — you proved you can tolerate uncertainty.
  • 5Increase daily: 20min → 1hr → 3hr → 24hr → never.
📋
Worst Case Acceptance Script
Face the feared outcome directly, without escape.
Imaginal Exposure
  • 1Write your worst fear in detail: "I have chest pain and it turns out to be a heart attack…"
  • 2Read it aloud slowly, 3 times. Let anxiety rise. Don't stop or reassure.
  • 3Notice the anxiety peak — then gradually decrease with each reading.
  • 4Repeat daily for 2 weeks with the same script. The fear loses power.
  • 5Goal: tolerating uncertainty — not convincing yourself it won't happen.
